Japanese Curry with Chicken Katsu

A recipe that I have to make at least once a month is Japanese Curry with Katsu Chicken. The glossy, creamy curry on top of the fluffy short-grain rice with a side of katsu chicken makes for an amazing meal. Anytime I make it for my family, there are never any leftovers. I first stumbled upon this video when I was on Instagram and decided that I wanted to make it. This was one of the first dishes that I served to my family. I had low expectations being that it was my first time making it, but I was pleasantly surprised by how amazing it turned out.


Ingredients


  •   1 lb chicken breast
  •   2 beaten eggs
  •   1 cup flour
  •   1 cup panko
  •   1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  •   2 large carrots, sliced
  •   1 apple, thinly slices
  •   3 pieces of garlic, smashed
  •   4 1/2 cups of chicken stock
  •   2 tablespoons curry powder
  •   4 tablespoons flour
  •   3 bay leaves
  •   1 tablespoon honey
  •   1 tablespoon soy sauce
  •   1 teaspoon garam masala
  •   2 cups short-grain rice
  •   2 cups water
  •   4 tablespoons rice vinegar
  •   1 tablespoon sugar
  •   1 teaspoon salt

Instructions


  1. Slice the chicken breast and pound them until they are thin. You can place a piece of parchment paper over the chicken so it does not smash into pieces. You can use a meat mallet, a frying pan, or a dough roller.

  2. Season beaten eggs and panko with salt, pepper, paprika, and garlic powder. Coat the chicken with flour, then the beaten eggs, and finally with the panko. Place on a rack until it’s time to fry the chicken.

  3. Heat oil in a dutch pot over medium heat and add your sliced onions, carrot, apple, and garlic for cover for 10 minutes.

  4. Add the curry powder and flour and gradually add in the chicken stock.

  5. Add bay leaves and simmer for 20 minutes while covered.

  6. Add honey, soy sauce and garam masala.

  7. Remove bay leaves and blend the curry with either an immersion blender or a normal blender. The curry is now ready.

  8. Wash the sushi rice until the water runs clear. Place the rice and water in a pot and heat until you see the water boiling. Cover the pot and simmer for 10 minutes. Turn the heat off and keep the rice covered for another 10 minutes to complete the cooking process.

  9. Place the cooked rice in a container and pour the rice vinegar on top. Using a wooden spatula, gently scoop rice from the slides to the center to make the rice fluffy. Try to not squish the rice.

  10. Heat some oil in a pan and shallow fry the chicken until golden brown.

  11. Plate and serve!
